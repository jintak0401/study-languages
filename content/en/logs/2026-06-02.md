# 2026-06-02 — used to: past habits

## Grammar focus

**used to + base verb** = a past habit or state that no longer exists.

| Form | Example |
|---|---|
| Past habit | I **used to** study English at school. |
| Past state | She **used to** live in Seoul. |
| Negative | I **didn't use to** be confident. |
| Question | **Did you use to** practise every day? |

### Don't confuse these two!

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **used to + verb** | past habit (no longer) | I used to be shy. |
| **be used to + -ing** | accustomed to (still true) | I am used to making mistakes. |

Common mistake: ~~I **use to** study~~ → correct: I **used to** study (the past form always has the 'd').

## Expressions

1. **I used to be nervous about speaking English, but now I enjoy it.**
   - 예전엔 긴장됐는데, 이제는 즐거워요.
   - Paraphrase: *I once found speaking scary, but I've grown to love it.*

2. **Lately, I've been struggling with pronunciation more than grammar.**
   - 요즘은 문법보다 발음이 더 힘들어요.
   - Paraphrase: *Recently, pronunciation has been harder for me than grammar.*
