# 2026-05-31 · Preposition + -ing

Today's rule: after a **preposition** (at, in, about, of, for, on…), the verb takes the **-ing** form — not the base verb.

## The pattern
- ❌ I'm good at **speak** English.
- ✅ I'm good at **speaking** English.

Common ones to memorize:
- **good at** + -ing → "She's good at cooking."
- **interested in** + -ing → "I'm interested in learning Japanese."
- **thank you for** + -ing → "Thank you for helping me."
- **think about** + -ing → "I'm thinking about changing jobs."

## New words
**fluent**, **awkward**, **get along with**, **come up with**, **eventually**.

## Conversation phrases
- "How do you say this in English?" — when you don't know a word.
- "Could you say that again, please?" — when you didn't catch something.
