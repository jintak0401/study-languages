# 2026-06-09 — Reported Speech

## Grammar: Reported speech — say (that) / tell someone (that)

When you report what someone said, **shift the tense back**:

| Direct speech | Reported speech |
|---|---|
| "I **like** English." | She said (that) she **liked** English. |
| "I **will** practise." | He said (that) he **would** practise. |
| "I **can** do it." | She told me she **could** do it. |
| "I **am** studying." | He said he **was** studying. |

**Key rule — say vs tell:**

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **say** | say + (that) + clause | She **said (that)** she was tired. |
| **tell** | tell + *person* + (that) + clause | She **told me (that)** she was tired. |

- ✅ She **told me** that she was studying.
- ❌ She **said me** that she was studying. ← `say` does NOT take a person object!

The word **that** is optional in everyday speech: "She said she was tired" is perfectly natural.

## New vocabulary

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **report** (v/n) | 보고하다 / 보고 |
| **inform** (v) | 알리다 |
| **claim** (v/n) | 주장하다 / 주장 |
| **deny** (v) | 부정하다 |
| **admit** (v) | 인정하다 |

## Practice expressions

1. *I heard that speaking every day is the fastest way to improve.*
2. *She told me that she would try speaking English with a stranger.*

## Tips

- **Shift pronouns too:** "I can come" → He said **he** could come.
- Common reporting verbs: say, tell, inform, mention, claim, deny, admit, report.
- **deny / admit** are followed by **-ing**: She denied **saying** it. He admitted **missing** the class.
