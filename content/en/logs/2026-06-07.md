## 2026-06-07 — Concession: although / even though vs. despite / in spite of

### Grammar focus

Two patterns for saying "X is true, but Y still happens":

| Pattern | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **although / even though** | + subject + verb | Although I was nervous, I spoke up. |
| **despite / in spite of** | + noun or -ing | Despite being nervous, I spoke up. |

> ❌ **Common mistake**: "Despite I was nervous..." — `despite` is a **preposition**, so it must be followed by a noun or -ing, never a full clause (subject + verb).

### Quick comparison

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|---|---|
| Although I make mistakes, I keep going. | Despite I make mistakes, I keep going. |
| Despite making mistakes, I keep going. | Despite make mistakes, I keep going. |
| Even though it's hard, I enjoy it. | Even though being hard, I enjoy it. |

### New vocabulary

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **despite** | ~에도 불구하고 (preposition) | Despite making errors, I kept talking. |
| **occasionally** | 가끔, 이따금 | I occasionally watch English films without subtitles. |
| **whereas** | 반면에 | I find grammar easy, whereas speaking is tough. |
| **regardless (of)** | 상관없이 | Keep going, regardless of how tired you feel. |
| **obstacle** | 장애물, 걸림돌 | Fear is the biggest obstacle to speaking. |

### Expressions

- **"Even though I make mistakes, I try to keep going."**
  → Despite making mistakes, I push myself to continue.
  → Although I make errors, I don't let them stop me.

- **"Despite being nervous, I tried speaking English with a stranger."**
  → Even though I was nervous, I spoke to a stranger in English.
  → In spite of feeling shy, I started the conversation.

### Key rule to remember

> **despite / in spite of** → always + **noun** or **-ing**
> **although / even though** → always + **subject + verb**
