# June 4 — Present Perfect: have/has + past participle

## What we covered

### Grammar: Present Perfect Tense

The present perfect connects the **past to now**. Use `have/has + past participle` in three situations:

| Use | Example |
|---|---|
| Life experience (no specific time) | I've visited Japan twice. |
| Recent event, still relevant now | I've just realised I was wrong. |
| Situation from the past, still true | I've studied English for five years. |

**Key rule**: if you name a finished time (yesterday, last year, in 2020), switch to **simple past**.

- ✅ I studied English last night. (finished time → simple past)
- ✅ I've studied English for five years. (still ongoing → present perfect)
- ❌ I studied English for five years. *(sounds like it's over)*
- ❌ I have studied English yesterday. *(finished time → wrong tense)*

### New Expressions

1. **"I've been studying English for about five years, but speaking is still the hardest part."**
   - 과거부터 지금까지 계속된 상황 → 현재완료 진행형 (`have been + -ing`)

2. **"Have you ever had a moment when everything just clicked?"**
   - `Have you ever + past participle` = 경험 묻기
   - *click* = 모든 것이 갑자기 맞아 떨어지는 느낌 (그 "아하!" 순간)

---

## Practice tip

When talking about yourself in English, ask: *"Is this still true now?"*
- If yes → **present perfect** (`I've lived here for 3 years.`)
- If no → **simple past** (`I lived there for a year, then I moved.`)
