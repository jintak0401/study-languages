# 2026-06-10 — Passive voice: be + past participle

## What we studied

Today's grammar focus: **the passive voice**.

In active sentences the subject performs the action. In passive sentences the subject receives it. You use the passive when:
- the action is more important than who did it
- the doer is unknown or obvious

**Form:**
```
subject + am / is / are + past participle          (present)
subject + was / were + past participle             (past)
subject + will be + past participle                (future)
```

To say who did the action, add **by + agent**:
> *This bridge **was built** by a French company.*

---

## Key rule: use the past participle — not the simple past

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---|---|
| The report was **wrote** by my boss. | The report was **written** by my boss. |
| The game was **broadcasted** live. | The game was **broadcast** live. |
| She was **hiren** last year. | She was **hired** last year. |

---

## Practice examples

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| A Korean company **made** this phone. | This phone **was made** by a Korean company. |
| People **speak** English everywhere. | English **is spoken** everywhere. |
| Someone **told** me the plan changed. | I **was told** the plan had changed. |
| They **will publish** the results Friday. | The results **will be published** Friday. |

---

## New vocabulary

| Word | Meaning | Passive example |
|---|---|---|
| **manufacture** (v) | 제조하다 | These cars are manufactured in Busan. |
| **broadcast** (v) | 방송하다 | The speech was broadcast on all channels. |
| **hire** (v) | 고용하다 | She was hired as a support specialist. |
| **publish** (v) | 출판하다; 발표하다 | His first novel was published at 24. |
| **replace** (v) | 교체하다 | The old laptop was replaced for free. |

---

## New expressions

> **"English is spoken all over the world."**
> 영어는 전 세계에서 쓰여요.

> **"This phone was made by a Korean company."**
> 이 폰은 한국 회사가 만들었어요.
