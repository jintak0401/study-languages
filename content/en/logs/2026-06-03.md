## Giving advice and suggestions

**Grammar focus: should / Why don't you / How about / You might want to**

Four natural ways to give advice and make suggestions — all very common in real conversation.

### Four suggestion patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| should + verb | You **should** review vocabulary every day. |
| Why don't you/we + verb? | **Why don't we** watch a drama without subtitles? |
| How about + -ing? | **How about keeping** a small vocabulary notebook? |
| You might want to + verb | **You might want to** slow down a little. |

### ⚠️ Watch out!

After **"Why don't you/we"**, use the **base verb** — no "to" after it:

- ❌ Why don't you **to try** speaking?
- ✅ Why don't you **try** speaking?

### New vocabulary today

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| suggest | 제안하다 |
| recommend | 추천하다, 권하다 |
| encourage | 격려하다, 권장하다 |
| remind | 상기시키다 |
| overcome | 극복하다 |

### Practice expressions

- *"Why don't we try watching English dramas without subtitles?"*
- *"You might want to keep a vocabulary notebook."*
