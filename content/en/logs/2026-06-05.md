# 2026-06-05 — 2nd Conditional: Imagining "What If"

## What we covered

Today's focus was the **2nd conditional** — used for unreal or imaginary situations in the present/future.

**Structure:** `If + past simple, would + base verb`

> If I had more time, I would study English every day.

The past tense in the if-clause does **not** mean past time — it signals the situation is hypothetical, not actually expected to happen.

---

## Key rule

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Common mistake |
|---|---|
| If I **had** more time, I **would** study. | If I **would have** more time, I **will** study. |
| What **would** you do if you **could** speak fluently? | What **will** you do if you **can** speak fluently? |

> **Never put "would" in the if-clause.**  
> The if-clause always uses the past simple (had, could, were, weren't…).

---

## New expressions

**"If I had more free time, I would immerse myself in English every day."**
시간이 더 있다면, 매일 영어에 완전히 빠져들 텐데요.

Paraphrases:
- If I weren't so busy, I'd dedicate more time to English every day.
- I wish I had more free time — I'd listen to English podcasts for hours.

---

**"What would you do if you could speak English perfectly?"**
영어를 완벽하게 말할 수 있다면 어떻게 하실 건가요?

Paraphrases:
- How would your life change if your English were perfect?
- Imagine you could speak English flawlessly — what would you do first?

---

## New vocabulary

| Word | Part of speech | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| afford (to do) | verb | (시간·돈의) 여유가 있다 |
| immerse (yourself in) | verb | 몰입하다, 완전히 빠져들다 |
| abroad | adverb | 해외에서, 외국에 |
| dedicate (time to) | verb | 전념하다, ~에 쏟아붓다 |
| pick up (a language) | phrasal verb | 자연스럽게 터득하다 |
