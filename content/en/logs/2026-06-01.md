# Giving Opinions — 2026-06-01

## Grammar focus: Expressing opinions naturally

Today's lesson covered how to share your views clearly and naturally in English.

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| I think (that)... | I think consistency matters more than long study sessions. |
| I believe... | I believe everyone can become fluent with the right habits. |
| In my opinion, | In my opinion, daily practice beats perfect grammar. |
| I'd say... | I'd say speaking with native speakers is the fastest way to learn. |
| From my perspective, | From my perspective, making mistakes is how we grow. |

**Common mistake to avoid:**  
❌ "My opinion is the daily practice is the best way to improve."  
✅ "In my opinion, daily practice is the best way to improve."

`In my opinion` and `From my perspective` go at the **start** of a sentence, followed by a comma. `I think / I believe / I feel` are followed directly by a noun clause.

## New vocabulary

| Word | Part of speech | Korean meaning |
|---|---|---|
| perspective | noun | 관점 |
| argue | verb | 주장하다, 논쟁하다 |
| reasonable | adjective | 합리적인 |
| convince | verb | 설득하다, 납득시키다 |
| tend to | phrase | ~하는 경향이 있다 |

> **Note on "argue":** In English, *argue* doesn't always mean fighting. "I would argue that…" = "주장하건대…" — a polite, academic way to give an opinion.

## New expressions

- **In my opinion, speaking every day is more important than perfect grammar.**  
  제 생각엔, 매일 말하는 게 완벽한 문법보다 더 중요해요.  
  Paraphrase: *From my perspective, consistent speaking beats perfect grammar.*

- **I tend to forget vocabulary if I don't review it.**  
  복습하지 않으면 단어를 잊어버리는 경향이 있어요.  
  Paraphrase: *Without review, I end up forgetting new vocabulary.*
