## 2026-06-11 — Question tags: isn't it? / don't you?

### What you learned today

**Grammar: Question tags**

A question tag is a short yes/no question added to the end of a statement. You use them to:
- **Check** information you think is true
- **Invite** the other person to agree

**The golden rule:** positive statement → negative tag; negative statement → positive tag.

| Statement | Tag | Full sentence |
|---|---|---|
| You study English (positive) | don't you? (negative) | You study English, **don't you?** |
| She has been here (positive) | hasn't she? (negative) | She has been here before, **hasn't she?** |
| It's not hard (negative) | is it? (positive) | It's not hard, **is it?** |
| They didn't cancel (negative) | did they? (positive) | They didn't cancel, **did they?** |

**The most common mistake:** Using *isn't it?* for everything (this is a direct translation from Korean 그렇죠/이죠). You must match the auxiliary verb in the tag to the main verb:

| Main verb | Auxiliary to use in tag |
|---|---|
| is / are / was / were | is/isn't, are/aren't, was/wasn't, were/weren't |
| do / does / did | do/don't, does/doesn't, did/didn't |
| have / has / had | have/haven't, has/hasn't, had/hadn't |
| can / will / would / should | can/can't, will/won't, would/wouldn't, should/shouldn't |

### New vocabulary

| Word | Part of speech | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| confirm | verb | 확인하다 | "Can you confirm the meeting is at 3?" |
| assume | verb | 가정하다 | "I assumed you were coming." |
| obvious | adjective | 명백한 | "That's obvious, isn't it?" |
| familiar | adjective | 익숙한 | "This sounds familiar, doesn't it?" |
| tag along | phrasal verb | 따라가다 | "You're joining us tonight, aren't you? Feel free to tag along." |

---

### Expressions to remember

- **"You've studied Japanese before, haven't you?"**
  Present perfect statement → use *haven't*, not *didn't* or *isn't it*.

- **"It's not as difficult as it looks, is it?"**
  Negative statement with *is* → positive tag *is it?* (not *isn't it?*).
