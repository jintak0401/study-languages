# Future plans: going to / planning to / intending to

## Grammar: be going to vs will

Use **be going to + base verb** for plans already decided *before* the moment of speaking:

> *I'm going to start a speaking course next month.* (already signed up)

Use **will + base verb** for spontaneous decisions made right now, or promises:

> *A: We're out of coffee.*
> *B: Don't worry, I'll grab some on the way home.* (decided just now → will)

**Natural alternatives for pre-decided plans:**

| Expression | Example |
|---|---|
| be going to | I'm going to join a language exchange group. |
| planning to | I'm planning to visit Japan next year. |
| thinking of + -ing | I'm thinking of signing up for an online class. |
| intending to | I intend to pass the English test this year. |

## Vocabulary

| Word | Part of speech | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| intend (to do) | verb | ~할 작정이다 |
| arrange | verb | 준비하다, 마련하다 |
| look forward to | phrasal verb | ~을 기대하다 (뒤에 noun/-ing!) |
| definitely | adverb | 분명히, 반드시 |
| upcoming | adjective | 다가오는, 곧 있을 |

## Expressions

- *I'm going to practise speaking every day from now on.*
- *I'm really looking forward to having a real conversation in English.*

## Watch out

**look forward to** is always followed by a **noun or -ing**, never a bare base verb:

❌ *I'm looking forward to meet you.*
✅ *I'm looking forward to **meeting** you.*

**going to** vs **will** — the test: did you decide this *before* now, or *right now*?

❌ *I will study abroad next year.* (you already booked — say *going to*)
✅ *I'm going to study abroad next year.*
✅ *I'll call you later.* (promise made just now — *will* is fine)
