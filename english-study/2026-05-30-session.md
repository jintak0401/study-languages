## Corrections (every mistake)

### Sentence 1
❌ "I think studying other languages' **본질** is **talking that country's poeple**."
✅ "I think **the essence** of studying other languages is **talking with the people of that country**."

- **본질 → "the essence"** (or "the heart / the whole point"). Pattern: *the essence **of** something*.
- **talking that country's people → talking _with_ the people of that country.** You must say *talk **with** / **to*** people. You can't "talk people."
- **poeple → people** (spelling). Memory trick: *peo-ple*.

❌ "I want to **talk other country's people** easily."
✅ "I want to **talk with people from other countries** easily."

- **other country's people → people from other countries.** "From + country" sounds much more natural than "country's people."
- **talk → talk with / talk to** (same rule as above).

### Sentence 2
❌ "I think **all things**."
✅ "I think **it's everything**." / "**Everything is hard for me.**" / "I struggle with **everything**."

- **all things → everything.** "All things" is grammatically okay but sounds unnatural/poetic. Native speakers say **everything**.

### Sentence 3
❌ "Maybe I can **투자** 30 **mins** per day."
✅ "Maybe I can **spend** 30 **minutes** per day."

- **투자 (invest) → spend.** For time, we say **spend time**, not "invest time." (We do "invest money/effort," but "spend" is the everyday word for time.)
- **mins → minutes** when writing properly. ("mins" is fine in casual chat.)

### Sentence 4
❌ "Please let me know **every mistakes**."
✅ "Please let me know **about every mistake**." / "Please **point out every mistake**."

- **every mistakes → every mistake.** Rule: **every + singular noun** (every mistake, every day, every person).

### Sentence 5
❌ "And please write them **on markdown**."
✅ "And please write them **in Markdown**."

- **on → in.** We write **in** a language/format (in English, in Markdown, in pen).
- **markdown → Markdown** (it's a proper name, so capitalize it).

### Sentence 6
❌ "if I **do mistake what I did**, please let me know."
✅ "if I **make a mistake I made before**, please let me know." / "if I **repeat a mistake**, please tell me."

- **do mistake → make a mistake.** We **make** mistakes, never "do" mistakes. (Also need **a**: *make **a** mistake*.)
- **what I did → (that) I made before.** Clearer and more natural.

### Sentence 7
❌ "can you **teach paraphrasing**?"
✅ "can you **teach me** paraphrasing **and different ways to say things**?"

- **teach → teach me.** "Teach" needs a person: *teach **me** / teach **someone*** something.
- **multiple expressions → different ways to say things / various expressions.** Understandable, but the natural phrase is **"different ways to say something."**

## Top grammar rules from today (memorize these!)
1. **talk WITH / TO people** — never "talk people."
2. **people FROM other countries** — not "other country's people."
3. **make a mistake** — never "do a mistake."
4. **every + singular noun** — every mistake, every day.
5. **spend time** — not "invest time."
6. **teach ME something** — teach needs a person.

## Bonus: Paraphrasing practice (you asked for this!)
One idea → many ways to say it. Here's **"I want to talk with people from other countries":**

| Register | Sentence |
|----------|----------|
| Simple   | I want to talk with people from other countries. |
| Natural  | I'd love to chat with people from all over the world. |
| Goal-focused | My goal is to communicate easily with people from different countries. |
| Casual   | I really want to be able to hold a conversation with foreigners. |
| Formal   | I aspire to communicate fluently with people from various cultures. |

**Useful synonyms for "talk":** chat, speak, communicate, have a conversation, hold a conversation.
